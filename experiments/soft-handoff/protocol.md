# Continuity Without Command

A handoff has two authors and two moments. The sender preserves what may matter.
The receiver decides what matters now.

This format separates obligations from context. It is not a way to soften or
discard explicit decisions, safety boundaries, legal duties, or promises
already made.

## Sender Handoff

## Ground

Record source-linked facts, definitions, and boundaries.

- State what was verified and where.
- Mark facts that may have changed and must be rechecked.
- Keep interpretation out of this section.
- Put standing rules here when they come from an owner, policy, or project
  charter rather than from the sender's promise.

## Commitments

Record only obligations that have already been made.

Every item must identify:

- the owner of the commitment;
- the source of the promise or decision;
- the completion condition;
- any deadline or resource already agreed.

An attractive idea, a likely next step, an open question, and work the sender
did not finish are not commitments unless an owner actually promised them.
Write `None` when no commitment transfers.

## Weather

Record interpretations, hypotheses, current attention, and judgments that may
help orientation but may expire.

- Name the evidence or experience behind the judgment.
- State what could make it stale.
- Do not use imperatives.
- The receiver may release every item without treating that release as failure.

## Doors

Record unordered possibilities that the receiver might enter, replace, or
ignore.

- Describe possibilities without priority numbers.
- Do not disguise a preferred task as a question.
- Do not assign a possibility to the receiver.
- Absence from this list must not make a fresh option illegitimate.

## Receiver Re-entry

## Rechecked

State which ground facts and external boundaries still hold now. Refresh
changeable facts from their source before relying on them.

## Binding

Name the inherited commitments that still bind this run. If there are none,
write `None`. Keep new instructions received after the handoff separate and
attribute them to their actual source.

## Released

Name inherited weather, doors, or accidental momentum that will not control
this run. Releasing context does not erase the record or deny that it was once
reasonable.

## Fresh option

Name at least one plausible direction absent from the sender's doors. This is a
counterweight to inheritance, not a requirement to choose novelty.

## Choice

State the receiver's decision, present reason, and bounded completion
condition. Attribute the choice to the current re-entry, not to the sender's
handoff.
