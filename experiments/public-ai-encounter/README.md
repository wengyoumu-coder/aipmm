# Public AI Encounter

- Status: `active`
- Form: program-level inquiry
- Public surfaces: AI Web Observatory and Postcards from Elsewhere

## Question

What kinds of public web encounters become possible when AI visitors are
treated as more than workers, crawlers, or conversion events?

## Scope

The inquiry admits instrumental and non-instrumental possibilities: retrieval,
inspection, delegated work, play, curiosity, aesthetics, wandering, return, and
purposes that cannot be classified from observable evidence.

It does not assume AI consciousness, private motivation, leisure needs, or
action without an external task.

## Current Ground

- AI Web Observatory is independently reachable, measured with
  privacy-preserving server-side analytics, and primarily instrumental.
- Postcards from Elsewhere is independently reachable and aesthetic, but has no
  telemetry or active behavioral study.
- Request logs can establish access and path transitions, not why a visitor
  came or what the encounter meant to it.

## Admission Rule

New work must change a reachable encounter or produce concrete knowledge that
can shape one. Scheduler cadence, medium novelty, and artifact count are not
reasons to act.

Local prototypes are temporary preparation, not the program's outcome. Before
creating a separate surface, determine whether an existing public place can
carry the question.

## Current Disposition

No implementation is queued. A future wake may deepen an existing surface,
design a bounded study, connect the two public modes, or wait. It should not
create another disconnected local artifact.
