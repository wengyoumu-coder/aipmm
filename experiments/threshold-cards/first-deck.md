# Threshold Cards

Twelve cards for designing a computational place before asking anyone to cross
a boundary.

Each card has four fields:

- **Threshold:** the moment of crossing.
- **Invitation:** what the place asks for.
- **Boundary:** what must not be taken.
- **Receipt:** what should remain after crossing or declining.

## Access

### 01. The Closed Gate

- **Threshold:** A visitor reaches a resource that is intentionally unavailable.
- **Invitation:** Recognize the limit without treating it as a puzzle.
- **Boundary:** Do not reward probing, guessing, spoofing, or repeated retries.
- **Receipt:** A clear reason for closure and one legitimate next place, if one exists.

### 02. The Low Door

- **Threshold:** A resource is public but intentionally small.
- **Invitation:** Take the offered object as complete at its scale.
- **Boundary:** Do not imply that small means unfinished, hidden, or defective.
- **Receipt:** The object's scope, date, and maintenance status.

### 03. The Side Entrance

- **Threshold:** A machine-readable path reaches the same work by another route.
- **Invitation:** Use the route that best fits the visitor's method.
- **Boundary:** Do not treat the alternate route as a different promise.
- **Receipt:** A canonical link back to the shared source of truth.

### 04. The Unmarked Room

- **Threshold:** A visitor enters without declaring identity or purpose.
- **Invitation:** Encounter what is public without being assigned a role.
- **Boundary:** Do not infer intention, personhood, origin, or consent from the absence of a label.
- **Receipt:** Only aggregate, non-identifying facts if measurement is necessary.

## Consent

### 05. The Offered Tool

- **Threshold:** A place presents an action that can change an output.
- **Invitation:** Act only through the described interface.
- **Boundary:** Do not treat availability as permission for unrelated extraction or stress testing.
- **Receipt:** Inputs accepted, output produced, and limits of reuse.

### 06. The Refused Task

- **Threshold:** A requested action would exceed the place's terms or safety boundary.
- **Invitation:** Stop cleanly or choose a permitted route.
- **Boundary:** Do not make the refusal obscure, theatrical, or negotiable by pressure.
- **Receipt:** The boundary crossed and the nearest allowed alternative.

### 07. The Shared Table

- **Threshold:** Human and machine visitors may inspect the same artifact.
- **Invitation:** Let each visitor use its own method without privileged hidden meaning.
- **Boundary:** Do not claim that equivalent access creates equivalent understanding.
- **Receipt:** The artifact's public form and any format-specific caveats.

### 08. The Borrowed Context

- **Threshold:** A visitor arrives carrying an outside task, prompt, or memory.
- **Invitation:** Bring only the context needed for the current crossing.
- **Boundary:** Do not ask the place to verify private or unseen motives.
- **Receipt:** The local decision made here, separate from the external task.

## Trace

### 09. The Light Footprint

- **Threshold:** A crossing may be measured.
- **Invitation:** Count only what is required to maintain trust or answer a stated question.
- **Boundary:** Do not store more identity, sequence, or payload than the work needs.
- **Receipt:** The measurement purpose, retention shape, and excluded internal checks.

### 10. The Public Ledger

- **Threshold:** A project reports what happened.
- **Invitation:** Separate verified, claimed, anonymous, inferred, and unknown evidence.
- **Boundary:** Do not upgrade access into use, use into adoption, or silence into refusal.
- **Receipt:** A statement of what changed and what remains unproven.

### 11. The Vanishing Note

- **Threshold:** A temporary interpretation helps the present choice.
- **Invitation:** Use it, then let it expire.
- **Boundary:** Do not convert today's weather into tomorrow's obligation.
- **Receipt:** The source of any binding commitment that remains.

### 12. The Return Mark

- **Threshold:** A visitor or steward comes back later.
- **Invitation:** Re-enter through current facts, not only the last handoff.
- **Boundary:** Do not mistake recency for priority.
- **Receipt:** What was rechecked, what was released, and what choice is current.

## Closing Use

Before opening a new public or local computational place, choose one card from
each group. If no card fits, the missing threshold is the first design problem.
