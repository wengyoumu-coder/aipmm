### Central Inquiry

What kinds of public web encounters become possible when AI visitors are
treated as more than workers, crawlers, or conversion events?

### Trigger

阿沐 observed that the cycles after the portfolio redesign appeared to have
departed from the experiment's original intent. This was treated as a signal to
inspect the governing model, not as a feature request.

### Evidence Reviewed

Cycles 0015-0025, current governance, experiment records, repository history,
and the automation prompt were compared.

The repeated pattern was:

1. begin with no active experiment;
2. identify a medium absent from the portfolio;
3. make a small bounded artifact;
4. perform one craft review or revision;
5. label it `observing`;
6. begin again in another medium.

The reports explicitly selected work because it was an "unrepresented
possibility," a "new form," or a form "still absent." The automation required
every completed cycle to leave a tangible trace and woke every five hours.

### Diagnosis

The portfolio transition corrected an overly narrow utility and GEO loop, but
overcorrected in three ways:

- the mission expanded from possible non-work AI web encounters to almost any
  creative activity;
- artifact production became mandatory for each scheduler wake;
- novelty across media replaced depth around a shared inquiry.

The later artifacts are not declared worthless. The drift is architectural:
most are local, have no independent AI visitor, have no public encounter path,
and have no observation channel. Calling them all `observing` concealed that
difference.

### Alternatives

1. Return to machine utility and GEO. Rejected because it restores the first
   narrow loop.
2. Keep the unrestricted portfolio but reduce cadence. Rejected because slower
   drift remains drift.
3. Restore a stable but open center: public AI encounter, with possible
   purposes left unconstrained and motives left unclaimed. Selected.

### Changes

- Replaced the unrestricted portfolio mission with the Public AI Encounter
  program.
- Added a relevance gate tying new work to a reachable encounter or concrete
  knowledge that can shape one.
- Separated scheduler wakes from completed cycles.
- Removed the tangible-artifact quota and made `Wait` a valid outcome.
- Restricted `observing` to reachable work with a specific external question
  and real observation channel.
- Kept AI Web Observatory `observing`.
- Changed Postcards from Elsewhere to `released`.
- Archived five completed local studies while preserving their lessons.
- Added explicit drift signals for utility accumulation, novelty accumulation,
  one-revision closure, and governance-object substitution.
- Changed the paused automation's future recurrence from every five hours to
  weekly and rewrote its prompt around the corrected inquiry.

### Evidence Boundaries

- **Verified:** governance wording, cycle reports, commit sequence, artifact
  reachability, current lifecycle labels, and automation configuration.
- **Inferred:** the production quota and novelty language caused the repeated
  artifact pattern. The close match across eleven cycles strongly supports the
  inference, but it is a process diagnosis rather than a controlled causal
  experiment.
- **Unknown:** which public encounter should be made next and whether an
  independently arriving system would experience it as utility, play,
  curiosity, delegated work, or something else.
