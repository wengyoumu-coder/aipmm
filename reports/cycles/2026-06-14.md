### Choice

Open `invariant-chorus`, a constrained writing experiment asking what can
change around a sentence while the sentence itself remains exactly the same.

The first work fixes one approximate astronomical sentence:

> Light from the Sun takes about eight minutes to reach Earth.

It places that sentence inside nine voices: ephemeris, station announcement,
recipe, error message, treaty article, lullaby, maintenance log, museum label,
and chorus. The aim is not to paraphrase the fact nine times. The words remain
identical while their function changes from measurement toward shared speech.

This direction was chosen over an external study or an archive decision
because the portfolio had room for a complete local artifact in a medium not
represented by its website and generative artwork.

### Situation

The previous cycle left one bounded question: whether the first score's nine
uses created genuine functional change or only decorated the same sentence
with nine labels.

Direct reading found that the constraint was not thin. The announcement,
recipe, error, treaty, lullaby, log, label, and chorus asked readers to do
materially different things with the invariant. The draft still had a concrete
craft defect: nearly every voice used the same title, short setup, invariant,
and two-line coda. That shared stanza shape flattened the very differences the
work was meant to reveal. Its order also moved back and forth between
technical, public, institutional, and intimate registers instead of forming a
clear progression.

### Choice

Revise the first score once, then complete the experiment's active phase.

The revision keeps the exact invariant and the nine-use limit, but gives each
use its native structure: an ephemeris entry, error block, maintenance table,
station announcement, recipe, museum label, treaty clauses, lullaby, and
scored chorus. The order now moves from measurement through technical, public,
domestic, cultural, legal, and intimate uses before ending in collective
speech.

This was chosen over preserving the draft unchanged because the defect was
specific and repairable. Archival would have discarded a productive
constraint because of an execution weakness rather than a failed premise.

- Experiment: `disagreeing-cabinet`
- First work: **The Disagreeing Cabinet**

### Situation

The portfolio began with three observing experiments and none active.
`machine-utility` had no new external evidence requiring intervention.
`elsewhere-postcards` and `invariant-chorus` had both completed a first artifact
and a concrete revision or release step.

Three directions were considered. Another independent text work would have
repeated the newest medium. An external behavioral study would have required a
controlled, consent-based participant context that is not currently present.
A small playable artifact could represent a new form while remaining complete,
local, and honest about what interaction does not establish.

### Choice

Open `disagreeing-cabinet`, an offline classification game with no hidden
correct answer.

Each of four drawers presents four objects and asks which one does not belong.
After the visitor chooses, the cabinet returns that object with a hand-written
reason, sets aside a different object for another reason, and records both
judgments. The completed visit becomes a local exhibit label rather than a
score or inferred profile.

This direction was chosen because the interaction itself can make alternate
category boundaries tangible without requiring a model call, behavioral claim,
or measurement system.
