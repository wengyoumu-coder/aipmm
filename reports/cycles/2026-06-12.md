- **Measurement gaps:** the repeated OAI claim could not move into verified

### Bottleneck Diagnosis

The long-running product constraint remains workflow retrieval to **Act**:
anonymous requesters retrieve useful workflow resources but do not call tools.
The strongest new cycle-specific constraint, however, was an evidence
qualification gap at **Discover / Return**. A repeated claimed crawler signal
appeared, but the experiment could not determine whether it represented a
legitimate search crawler or a spoofed User-Agent.

### Candidate Actions

Scores are 1 to 5. For cost, 5 means lowest cost.

| Type | Candidate | Mission impact | Evidence fit | Learning value | Reversibility | Cost |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Exploit | Strengthen robots and sitemap continuation after the repeated robots fetch | 4 | 5 | 3 | 5 | 5 |

| Measure | Verify future claimed OpenAI requests against official IP ranges during recording | 5 | 5 | 5 | 5 | 4 |

The measure option was selected because a real repeated claim had appeared and
could not be trusted. Changing discovery surfaces based on an unverified
identity would risk optimizing for spoofed behavior. The public verification
tool was deferred to keep the bet focused on evidence integrity.

### Hypothesis

If future claimed OpenAI requests are checked against OpenAI's official
published IPv4 ranges at request time, the experiment can distinguish verified
network-origin signals from claims without storing raw IP addresses.

The expected future evidence is an `OAI-SearchBot`, `GPTBot`, or
`ChatGPT-User` request recorded as `verified`, `not_verified`,
`source_unavailable`, or `unsupported`. Continued claims marked
`not_verified`, or repeated source failures, would weaken confidence in the
claimed crawler signal. No historical request would be retroactively labeled.

Safety constraints remained unchanged: no raw IP storage, synthetic organic
requests, spoofed identities, third-party promotion, or internal checks counted
as behavior.

### Actions

- Added a focused IPv4 CIDR verifier for the official OpenAI searchbot, GPTBot,
  and ChatGPT-User range sources.
- Added request-time network verification that fails closed to
  `source_unavailable` without dropping the analytics event.
- Added three privacy-safe D1 fields for verification status, source URL, and
  source version timestamp. Raw client IP addresses remain unpersisted.
- Added `verifiedAiRequests` and an aggregate `identity_verification` dataset
  to `npm run report:raw`.
- Added verified AI request count to `npm run cycle:context`.
- Applied the additive production migration. Existing rows defaulted to
  `not_checked`, preserving historical uncertainty.
