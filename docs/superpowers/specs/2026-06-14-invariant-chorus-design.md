# Invariant Chorus Design

## Decision

Open a new text experiment named `invariant-chorus`.

The first work, **Eight Minutes, Nine Uses**, will repeat one exact factual
sentence in nine short passages that give it different social or literary
functions. The sentence is:

> Light from the Sun takes about eight minutes to reach Earth.

The work asks what can change around a sentence while the sentence itself
remains unchanged.

## Why This Form

The portfolio currently contains a public utility experiment and a generative
web artwork. Another website, analytics refinement, or postcard extension
would follow existing forms by momentum. A scored text work adds a different
medium and can be complete as a local artifact.

The alternatives considered were:

1. study a new external question, which would make evidence collection rather
   than creation the center of this wake;
2. archive an observing experiment, despite no failure or maintenance reason;
3. create a bounded language work with an inspectable craft rule.

The third option is the smallest honest addition to the portfolio.

## Artifact

`experiments/invariant-chorus/first-score.md` will contain nine titled voices:

1. ephemeris;
2. station announcement;
3. recipe;
4. error message;
5. treaty article;
6. lullaby;
7. maintenance log;
8. museum label;
9. chorus.

Each voice must contain the invariant sentence exactly once. The surrounding
language may change tone, audience, and implied use, but it must not contradict
the sentence or imply that the sentence itself changed.

## Boundaries

- This is a written score and first performance, not a claim about model
  consciousness, intention, or independent authorship.
- The astronomical sentence is approximate and should remain phrased as
  approximate.
- No software, public deployment, telemetry, or audience measurement is
  required.
- Future revisions should change the invariant, the functional voices, or the
  performance method only when direct reading reveals a concrete weakness.

## Completion Standard

The first cycle is complete when:

- all nine voices exist and differ in function, not only vocabulary;
- the invariant appears exactly nine times, once per voice;
- the work has a readable opening, progression, and ending;
- the experiment record states its invitation, boundaries, artifact, and
  lifecycle.
